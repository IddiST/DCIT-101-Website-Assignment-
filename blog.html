The Kakum Conservation Area

History Of Kakum conservation area

Posted by Iddris Saeed Tipagya

Kakum conservation Area(KCA), comprising the Kakum National Park and Assin Attandanso Resource Reserve, is one of the 16 protected areas established, and being managed by the Wildlife Division of the Forestry Commission of Ghana. The two protected areas are gazetted by Legislative Instrument 1525, 1991. KCA covers an area of 360 km? (36000 hectares) and lie between longitudes 10 30' and 10 51 W and latitudes 50 20' and 50 40'N found in the Twifo Hemang Lower Denkyira and Assin South districts in the Central Region of Ghana. The conservation area is surrounded by 52 settlements and hamlets with a population of about 100,000 and located within the Twifo Hemang, Assin Attandanso, Assin Apimanim, Effutakwa Denkyira and Abura traditional areas.

Protected Area

Protected areas (PAs) are clearly defined geographical space, recognized, dedicated and managed, through legal and other effective means, to achieve the long term conservation of nature, associated ecosystem services and cultural values. Forest protected areas help conserve ecosystems that provide habitat, shelter, food, raw material, genetic materials, a barrier against disasters, and many other ecosystem goods and services and thus can have important role in helping species, people and countries adapt to climate change.


Tropical Rain Forest

It is a representative sample of fast disappearing tropical rainforests and serve as habitat for forest species of global conservation concern such as forest elephants, colobus monkeys, long snouted and dwarf crocodiles, and Hypolycaena kakumi (a butterfly species first discovery to science in (KCA)among others. KCA being well drained serves as the watershed for major streams such as Kakum, Obuo and Afia which provide drinking water for people in the Cape Coast Metropolis, Twifo Hemang Lower Denkyira and Assin South districts. The KCA provides a unique microclimate suitable for agriculture and as a result it is surrounded by a mosaic of farms such as oil palm, cocoa and rubber plantations. Being an ecotourism destination, CA receives over one hundred thousand tourists annually making it the most subscribed protected area in Ghana.

Number of Challenges

In spite of its importance, KCA faces a number of challenges such as hunting of wildlife including wholly protected species, illegal logging, farm encroachments and fringe land conversion for food and cash crops. Moreover, with KCA lacking buffer zones around the park and thus sharing boundary with farms, the level of elephant crop raiding on fringe farm sources is pronounced. This often leads to various levels of human wildlife conflicts that negatively affects livelihood security of fringe communities and impact on the PA's conservation efforts

Tourism Products

Current tourism products at Kakum National Park include Canopy Walkway, Bird Watching, Camping, Tree House accommodation and Nature Walk or Hiking.



A time series historical perspective of Kakum Conservation Area 1925 - 1926 Kakum Forest Reserve covering an area of 213.48 km2 was selected and demarcated by the Gold Coast Gazette. The Governor in Council at the time, constituted the demarcated area into a forest reserve, under section 4(4) of the Forest Ordinance, Cap 63. The reserve was necessary to protect the forest against destruction since it acts as a safeguard for the water supply of the region and the climatic conditions necessary for productive agriculture
